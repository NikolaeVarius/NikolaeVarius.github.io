About This Site

I created this website in order to flex my web development and programming skills. Initially I tried out creating this site using Ruby on Rails but I realized that I was being too ambitious to go straight into a dynamic website. Most of what I wanted to do could be done perfectly fine on a static website, and I wouldn't need to try and figure out how to jury-rig a fully static site into your normal MVC run website.

Instead I decided to use Jekyll, a Ruby gem that uses a combination of Ruby, Markdown, YAML, and Liquid Templating Engine, to facilitate the create of a static website.

Design Language

I've never been formally trained on design. Truthfully as a classic engineer, I've always eschewed design in favor of functionality. However, I am a sucker for consistancy of design. But even then, saying that, this website is a bit of a sandbox for me, which is why there are sometimes vastly different designs on pages. It's all in good fun.

The design of this site is based off Google's Material Design Language, the design that Google is using as their default starting from Android 5.0 Lollipop and with their Google Apps suite.

My opinions on this design schema are mixed. While I'm' a fan of how it is designed to make information readiliy available through the use of bold colors and large typography, the "stock" Google design has a bit too much whitespace for my liking. Information density seems to greatly suffer as a result.

Also specifically in terms of mobile, the use of such bright colors as well as a white background as a base tends to drain battery quickly from phones using AMOLED screens, which many flagship Android phones use.
